# 4.3 意见表达与建议 (Expressing Opinions and Making Suggestions)

## 学习目标 Learning Objectives

- 掌握15个与意见表达和建议相关的B1级核心词汇
- 学会用英语表达同意和不同意
- 掌握情态动词 should / could / might 在建议中的用法
- 能够用英语参与讨论并给出合理的建议

## 实用句型 Useful Sentences

1. **I think that we should focus on quality rather than quantity.**
   我认为我们应该关注质量而非数量。

2. **In my opinion, this is not the best approach.**
   在我看来，这不是最好的方法。

3. **I agree with you, but we also need to consider the budget.**
   我同意你的看法，但我们也需要考虑预算。

4. **I'm sorry, but I disagree. I have a different point of view.**
   抱歉，我不同意。我有不同的看法。

5. **Could you tell me the reason for your decision?**
   你能告诉我你做出这个决定的原因吗？

6. **I recommend that we try a different strategy.**
   我建议我们尝试不同的策略。

7. **It's important to get everyone's opinion before making a decision.**
   在做决定之前听取每个人的意见是很重要的。

8. **I prefer Option A because it's more cost-effective.**
   我更喜欢方案A，因为它更具成本效益。

## 语法要点 Grammar Point

### 情态动词 should / could / might 用于建议 (Modal Verbs for Suggestions)

**1. Should — 用于给出强烈的建议或意见 (应该)**
- You **should** talk to your manager about this issue.
  你应该和你的经理谈谈这个问题。
- We **should** arrive early to get good seats.
  我们应该早点到，以便找到好位置。
- You **should not** (shouldn't) ignore your colleagues' suggestions.
  你不应该忽视同事的建议。

**2. Could — 用于提出较为温和的建议或可能性 (可以)**
- You **could** try asking for help from your teammates.
  你可以尝试向队友寻求帮助。
- We **could** have the meeting in the afternoon instead.
  我们可以改在下午开会。
- **Could** we discuss this later? (礼貌建议推迟)
  我们能晚点再讨论这个吗？

**3. Might — 用于提出不太确定或更委婉的建议 (也许可以；可能)**
- You **might** want to check the details again.
  你也许想再检查一下细节。
- It **might** be a good idea to prepare a backup plan.
  准备一个备用计划也许是个好主意。
- That **might** work, but I'm not entirely sure.
  那也许可行，但我不太确定。

### I think that... 句型 (Expressing Personal Opinion)

结构：I think (that) + 从句

- I **think that** this is the best option.
- I **think** we should wait a little longer.
- I don't **think** that's a good idea. (注意：否定在主句)

### 礼貌表达不同意的技巧

- I see your point, but... (我理解你的观点，但是……)
- That's a good idea, however... (这是个好主意，不过……)
- I respect your opinion, but I feel differently. (我尊重你的意见，但我的感觉不同。)

## 对话练习 Conversation Practice

**对话：讨论工作安排 (Discussing Work Arrangements)**

A: We need to decide how to organize the team meeting next week. What do you think, Lisa?

B: Well, I think that we should have it on Tuesday morning. Most people are more focused at that time.

A: That's a good point. But I feel that Monday afternoon might be better because we can review the weekend report first.

B: I see your point, but in my opinion, Monday mornings are usually very busy. People have a lot of emails to catch up on.

A: That's true. So you prefer Tuesday morning?

B: Yes, I recommend we set it at 10 a.m. on Tuesday. What do you think?

A: I agree with the time, but could we consider Thursday instead? Some team members have other meetings on Tuesdays.

B: That's possible. Let me check everyone's schedule first. It's important to find a time that works for all of us.

A: I completely agree. We should also prepare an agenda before the meeting.

B: Absolutely. I'll take care of that.
