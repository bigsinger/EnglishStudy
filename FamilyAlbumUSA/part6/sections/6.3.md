# 6.3 第二十三集：The Big Apple

---

## 一、剧情概要

本集是《走遍美国》（Family Album USA）第二十三集，标题为 **"The Big Apple"**——这是纽约市最著名的别称。故事围绕主人公们前往纽约展开，展现了这座世界级大都市的独特魅力。

在这一集中，角色们游览了纽约的多个标志性地点，体验了这座城市的交通方式、文化氛围和生活节奏。通过他们的对话，观众可以学习到如何用英语问路、指路、描述城市景观，以及如何转述他人的话语。本集的核心场景包括：

- 在纽约街头询问路线
- 乘坐纽约地铁和出租车
- 参观时代广场（Times Square）、中央公园（Central Park）、百老汇（Broadway）等地标
- 讨论纽约博物馆和画廊

通过这集故事，学习者不仅能掌握实用的城市旅行英语，还能了解纽约"大苹果"这一文化符号的由来和意义。

## 二、经典对话

### 对话一：在时代广场问路

> **背景：Richard 和 Susan 在时代广场迷路，向一位路人问路。**

| 角色 | 对话 |
|------|------|
| Richard | **Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to the Museum of Modern Art?** |
| 路人 | **Sure. It's not far from here. Go straight two blocks, then turn left on Fifth Avenue. You'll see it on your right.** |
| Richard | **Go straight two blocks, turn left on Fifth Avenue. Is it within walking distance?** |
| 路人 | **Absolutely. It's about a ten-minute walk. You could also take the subway, but walking is better — you'll see Times Square along the way.** |
| Susan | **Thank you so much. We're visiting New York for the first time. The city is amazing!** |
| 路人 | **Enjoy your stay in the Big Apple!** |

**中文翻译：**
- Richard：打扰一下，您能告诉我怎么去现代艺术博物馆吗？
- 路人：当然可以。离这儿不远。直走两个街区，然后在第五大道左转。它就在你的右手边。
- Richard：直走两个街区，在第五大道左转。走路能到吗？
- 路人：完全可以。大约步行十分钟。你也可以坐地铁，但步行更好——沿途你会看到时代广场。
- Susan：非常感谢。我们第一次来纽约。这座城市太棒了！
- 路人：祝你们在大苹果玩得开心！

### 对话二：乘坐纽约地铁

> **背景：Harry 和 Susan 讨论如何游览纽约，选择交通方式。**

| 角色 | 对话 |
|------|------|
| Harry | **What's the best way to see New York? Should we take a taxi?** |
| Susan | **I think we should take the subway. It's faster and cheaper. Besides, riding the subway is a real New York experience.** |
| Harry | **But isn't the subway confusing for tourists?** |
| Susan | **Not really. He said that the subway map is very clear. She told me that all the stations are marked with different colors.** |
| Harry | **Alright, let's take the subway to Times Square. Then we can walk to Broadway from there.** |
| Susan | **Good idea. The Broadway theaters are amazing. I've heard that the shows are world-class.** |
| Harry | **And after that, maybe we can take a taxi to Central Park.** |

**中文翻译：**
- Harry：游览纽约最好的方式是什么？我们该打车吗？
- Susan：我觉得我们应该坐地铁。更快也更便宜。而且，坐地铁是真正的纽约体验。
- Harry：但是地铁对游客来说不是很难看懂吗？
- Susan：不会的。他说地铁路线图非常清晰。她告诉我所有车站都用不同颜色标记了。
- Harry：好吧，我们坐地铁去时代广场。然后从那儿可以走到百老汇。
- Susan：好主意。百老汇的剧院太棒了。我听说那些演出是世界一流的。
- Harry：之后，也许我们可以打车去中央公园。

### 对话三：谈论纽约地标

> **背景：一家人坐在中央公园的长椅上，谈论今天的见闻。**

| 角色 | 对话 |
|------|------|
| Richard | **I can't believe we're finally in New York City! Look at those skyscrapers!** |
| Susan | **She told me that the Empire State Building is one of the most famous landmarks in the world.** |
| Richard | **And she also said that we should visit the art galleries in Chelsea. There are so many museums here.** |
| Susan | **New York is called the Big Apple. Do you know why?** |
| Richard | **Someone told me that jazz musicians in the 1920s started calling it that. They said that New York was the "big apple" of success.** |
| Susan | **That's right. And now it's a symbol of the city — the city of opportunity, culture, and excitement.** |
| Richard | **I love this city. There's so much to see and do!** |

**中文翻译：**
- Richard：真不敢相信我们终于来到纽约了！看那些摩天大楼！
- Susan：她告诉我帝国大厦是全世界最著名的地标之一。
- Richard：她还说我们应该去切尔西的美术馆看看。这里博物馆太多了。
- Susan：纽约被称为大苹果。你知道为什么吗？
- Richard：有人告诉我那是20世纪20年代的爵士乐手们开始这么叫的。他们说纽约是成功的"大苹果"。
- Susan：没错。现在它是这座城市的象征——充满机遇、文化和激情的城市。
- Richard：我爱这座城市。有太多可以看和做的事情了！

## 五、语法要点：间接引语 / 转述句（Reported Speech）

### 5.1 什么是间接引语？

间接引语（Reported Speech）用于转述别人说过的话，而不直接引用原话。与直接引语（Direct Speech）不同，间接引语不需要引号，通常使用 **say, tell, ask** 等动词引导，并且时态、人称、时间状语等会发生相应变化。

### 5.2 直接引语 vs. 间接引语对比

| 直接引语（Direct Speech） | 间接引语（Reported Speech） |
|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| He said, **"The subway map is clear."** | He said **that the subway map was clear.** |
| She said, **"The station is near here."** | She said **that the station was near there.** |
| He said, **"I will take the subway."** | He said **that he would take the subway.** |
| She said, **"I have visited the museum."** | She said **that she had visited the museum.** |
| He said, **"I am going to Times Square."** | He said **that he was going to Times Square.** |

### 5.3 本集中的间接引语

以下是本集对话中出现的间接引语句子，非常值得学习：

1. **He said that the subway map is very clear.**
   - 他说地铁路线图非常清晰。
   - *注意：当转述的内容仍然是事实时，时态可以保持不变。*

2. **She told me that all the stations are marked with different colors.**
   - 她告诉我所有车站都用不同颜色标记了。

3. **She told me that the Empire State Building is one of the most famous landmarks in the world.**
   - 她告诉我帝国大厦是全世界最著名的地标之一。

4. **She also said that we should visit the art galleries in Chelsea.**
   - 她还说我们应该去切尔西的美术馆看看。

5. **Someone told me that jazz musicians in the 1920s started calling it that.**
   - 有人告诉我那是20世纪20年代的爵士乐手们开始这么叫的。

6. **They said that New York was the "big apple" of success.**
   - 他们说纽约是成功的"大苹果"。

### 5.4 时态变化规则

| 直接引语时态 | 间接引语时态 | 示例 |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| 一般现在时 | 一般过去时 | "I **like** New York" → He said he **liked** New York. |
| 现在进行时 | 过去进行时 | "I **am walking**" → He said he **was walking**. |
| 一般将来时 | 过去将来时 | "I **will go**" → He said he **would go**. |
| 现在完成时 | 过去完成时 | "I **have seen** it" → He said he **had seen** it. |
| 一般过去时 | 过去完成时 | "I **saw** it" → He said he **had seen** it. |

### 5.5 时间状语的变化

| 直接引语 | 间接引语 |
|----------|----------|
| now | then |
| today | that day |
| tonight | that night |
| this week | that week |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| here | there |
| ago | before |

### 5.6 say vs. tell 的区别

- **say + (that) + 从句**：He said **(that)** the city is beautiful.
- **tell + 人 + (that) + 从句**：He **told me** (that) the city is beautiful.

注意：**tell** 后面必须跟宾语（告诉谁），而 **say** 后面可直接跟从句。

## 六、文化注释

### 6.1 "The Big Apple" 的由来

"The Big Apple" 是纽约市最著名的昵称，其起源可以追溯到20世纪初期：

- **1920年代爵士乐时期**：爵士乐手们将纽约称为"大苹果"，意思是"成功的最大奖赏"。当时的俗语"There are many apples on the tree, but only one Big Apple" 意为树上有许多苹果，但只有一个大苹果——纽约就是那个机会最多最大的城市。
- **1970年代推广**：纽约市旅游局在1970年代正式将"Big Apple"作为官方宣传口号，旨在吸引游客，重振纽约的旅游业。
- **文化象征**：如今，"大苹果"已经成为纽约的同义词，代表机遇、多元文化、艺术和无限可能。

### 6.2 纽约地标文化

| 地标 | 英文 | 文化意义 |
|------|------|----------|
| **时代广场** | Times Square | 纽约最繁华的广场，以巨大的电子广告牌和百老汇剧院区闻名。每年新年前夜的"落球"（Ball Drop）庆典举世闻名。 |
| **中央公园** | Central Park | 位于曼哈顿中心的城市绿洲，占地843英亩，是纽约人休闲娱乐的首选之地。 |
| **百老汇** | Broadway | 世界戏剧中心，汇集了数十家剧院，上演《狮子王》《汉密尔顿》等经典音乐剧。 |
| **帝国大厦** | Empire State Building | 纽约最标志性的摩天大楼，高443米，观景台可俯瞰全城。 |
| **自由女神像** | Statue of Liberty | 法国送给美国的礼物，是自由和民主的象征。 |
| **大都会博物馆** | Metropolitan Museum of Art | 世界三大博物馆之一，收藏超过200万件艺术品。 |
| **第五大道** | Fifth Avenue | 全球最著名的购物街之一，汇集了众多奢侈品牌旗舰店。 |

### 6.3 纽约的交通文化

- **黄色出租车（Yellow Taxi）**：纽约的标志性符号之一，大街小巷随处可见的黄色出租车是纽约的城市名片。
- **24小时地铁**：纽约地铁是全球少数几个24小时运营的地铁系统之一，共有472个车站，覆盖全市五个行政区。
- **步行文化**：纽约是步行者的城市，很多地标之间步行即可到达，这也是体验纽约的最佳方式之一。

### 6.4 纽约的博物馆与艺术

纽约拥有世界顶级的博物馆和画廊文化：
- **大都会艺术博物馆（The Met）**
- **现代艺术博物馆（MoMA）**
- **古根海姆博物馆（Guggenheim Museum）**
- **切尔西画廊区（Chelsea Galleries）**

## 八、答案

### 练习一：填空

1. Go straight two **blocks**, then turn left.
2. The New York **subway** runs 24 hours a day.
3. He **said** that the museum was very famous.
4. She **told** me that she had visited the art gallery.
5. New York is called the **Big Apple**.

### 练习二：直接引语转间接引语

1. He said **that the museum was near there.**
2. She said **that she would take the subway.**
3. Richard said **that he loved New York City.**
4. Susan told me **that Times Square was amazing.**
5. The guide said **that the skyscrapers were very tall.**

### 练习三：阅读理解与表达

1. **New York is called "The Big Apple" because jazz musicians in the 1920s started calling it that. They said that New York was the "big apple" of success — meaning it was the biggest prize and the best place to succeed.**
2. **The episode mentions several ways to travel around New York: walking, taking the subway, and taking a taxi.**
3. **Famous landmarks in New York include: Times Square, Central Park, Broadway, the Empire State Building, the Statue of Liberty, and the Museum of Modern Art.**

### 练习四：情景对话（参考示例）

| 角色 | 对话 |
|------|------|
| Tourist | **Excuse me, could you tell me how to get to Central Park?** |
| Local | **Sure. Go straight for three blocks, then turn right on Fifth Avenue. Walk for another five minutes and you'll see the park entrance on your left.** |
| Tourist | **Go straight three blocks, turn right on Fifth Avenue. Is it far from here?** |
| Local | **No, it's about a fifteen-minute walk. You could take the subway too, but walking is more enjoyable.** |
| Tourist | **Thank you so much. Someone told me that Central Park is beautiful in the fall. I can't wait to see it!** |
